About me

Elias never planned on becoming the unofficial “fixer” of his neighborhood, but somehow he ended up being the one everyone called when a bike chain snapped, a fence leaned too far, or a stubborn jar lid refused to budge. He carried a pocketful of mismatched screws and a notebook filled with half-sketched inventions, always tinkering with ideas that might make life a little smoother. Though he rarely sought the spotlight, people noticed the way his eyes lit up when he solved a problem—like he’d just uncovered a secret puzzle piece the world had been missing. To him, every small repair was less about the object itself and more about keeping the rhythm of daily life humming along.
